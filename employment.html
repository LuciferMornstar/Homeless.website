Employment Resources

Finding stable employment is a crucial step in breaking the cycle of homelessness. Our employment resources are designed to help you develop skills, find job opportunities, and build a sustainable career.

Whether you need help with your CV, interview preparation, skills training, or connecting with employers who understand your situation, we're here to support you every step of the way.

Job Search Services

CV & Application Support

Our trained advisors can help you create or update your CV, write compelling cover letters, and complete job applications effectively.

Available at community centers across the UK or remotely via phone/online.

Interview Preparation

Practice interview skills, receive feedback, and gain confidence through mock interviews with our volunteer professionals.

We can also provide interview clothing through our partner organizations.

Employer Partnerships

We collaborate with understanding employers who offer inclusive hiring practices and supportive work environments.

These partnerships include flexible working arrangements to accommodate your unique circumstances.

Training & Education

Developing relevant skills is essential for long-term employment success. We offer various training programs to help you build marketable skills for today's job market.

Vocational Training

  • • Construction & trades (CSCS card certification)
  • • Food hygiene & hospitality
  • • Care work & healthcare support
  • • IT skills & digital literacy
  • • Retail & customer service

Courses run regularly throughout the year at various locations or online.

Soft Skills Workshops

  • • Communication & teamwork
  • • Time management
  • • Work readiness
  • • Confidence building
  • • Financial literacy

Weekly workshops available at community centers or remotely via video call.

Educational Support

For those looking to further their education, we can help with:

  • • College application assistance
  • • Access to course materials and laptops
  • • Information on grants and financial support
  • • Mentoring throughout your educational journey

Overcoming Employment Barriers

We understand that finding and maintaining employment can be challenging when experiencing homelessness. Our comprehensive support addresses common barriers:

Address & Documentation

Use our address service for job applications, and get help obtaining essential identification documents like ID cards, birth certificates, and NI numbers.

Transportation

Access to transportation assistance for interviews and the initial weeks of employment, including bus passes, bike loan schemes, and travel planning support.

Digital Access

Use our computer centers with internet access for job searches, or borrow devices through our technology lending program for remote applications and interviews.

Ongoing Employment Support

Employment Buddies

Our volunteer employment buddies provide ongoing support during your first months in a new job, helping you navigate workplace challenges and build confidence.

They offer encouragement, practical advice, and help with work-related problem-solving.

Benefits Advice

Transitioning from benefits to employment can be complex. Our advisors help you understand how work affects your benefits and ensure you're accessing all available support.

We can also assist with tax issues and Universal Credit management.

Career Progression

Our support doesn't end when you find a job. We offer ongoing career coaching to help you:

  • • Identify opportunities for advancement
  • • Develop new skills to progress in your career
  • • Navigate workplace challenges
  • • Build long-term career resilience

Success Stories

David's Journey

"After 8 months of homelessness, I connected with the employment team who helped me update my CV and get my CSCS card. Within three weeks, I secured a construction job and have been working steadily for over a year. The ongoing support helped me manage my finances and eventually move into my own flat."

Sarah's Path

"I never thought I could work in healthcare with my background. The team helped me access a care assistant course and connected me with an employer who gave me a chance. My employment buddy helped me through the first difficult months, and two years later, I'm now training to be a healthcare support worker."
