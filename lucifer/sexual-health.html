The Ultimate UK Guide: Sexual Health & Safety While Homeless

Comprehensive, practical, and trauma-informed support for everyone—no matter your situation or ability.

Get Help NowView Resources

Quick Summary (Easy Read)

  • Sex is normal. You deserve to be safe and healthy, even if you are homeless.
  • Use condoms every time. Get them free from NHS clinics and charities.
  • Ask for help if you feel unsafe or worried. You have rights and support is available.
  • Service dogs can help with mental health and safety.
  • Contact us or the numbers below if you need urgent help.

Understanding the Challenges

  • Lack of privacy and safe spaces for intimacy
  • Limited access to condoms, lubricant, and healthcare
  • Increased risk of STIs and sexual assault
  • Barriers to services (ID, stigma, fear, mental health, substance use)
  • Survival sex and vulnerability to exploitation
  • Mental health and substance use can make things harder

Homelessness makes it harder to stay safe and healthy. This guide helps you protect yourself and your partners, understand your rights, and find support—no matter your situation or ability.

Finding Safe Spaces

  • Shelters: Some allow couples or have private areas. Ask about policies. Some are dog-friendly.
  • Temporary Accommodation: Council-provided places may offer more privacy—check locks and safety.
  • Public Spaces: If you must, choose well-lit, safer areas and know the legal risks.
  • Hotels/Motels: Pooling resources can help, but check for safety and avoid exploitation.
  • Community Groups: Local organisations may help you find safe spaces or advocate for you.
  • Sexual Health Clinics: Offer confidential, private consultations and support.

Protecting Your Health

  • Use condoms (male or female) every time. Free from NHS clinics, charities, and outreach.
  • Use water-based lubricant to prevent condom breakage.
  • Get regular STI tests—even if you have no symptoms. Free and confidential at NHS clinics.
  • Vaccinations for HPV and hepatitis B are available on the NHS.
  • Emergency contraception and HIV prevention (PrEP/PEP) are available—ask at clinics or A&E.

Common STIs: What You Need to Know

  • Chlamydia: Often no symptoms. Can cause infertility. Easily treated with antibiotics.
  • Gonorrhoea: Painful urination, discharge. Can cause serious complications if untreated.
  • Syphilis: Serious if untreated. Treated with penicillin.
  • HIV: Attacks the immune system. No cure, but treatment allows a long, healthy life.
  • Genital Herpes: Painful blisters. No cure, but treatment helps.
  • Genital Warts: Caused by HPV. Can be treated.
  • Hepatitis B: Affects the liver. Vaccination available.

Get tested regularly. Most STIs are treatable, and early treatment protects you and others.

Legal Considerations in the UK

  • Consent: Must be voluntary and informed. If in doubt, say no. The law protects you.
  • Public Sex: Illegal and can lead to arrest. Know the risks.
  • Sex Work: Not illegal, but related activities (soliciting, brothels, pimping) are. Be aware of the law and your rights.
  • Reporting Assault: Call police or Rape Crisis (0808 802 9999). NHS SARCs offer free, confidential help.
  • Exploitation: The law protects you from being forced or tricked into sex or sex work.

If you feel unsafe or have been assaulted, get help as soon as possible. Your safety and dignity matter. All information is based on British law.

Mental Health & Substance Use

  • Mental health and substance use can affect decision-making and safety.
  • Support is available—ask for trauma-informed care and mental health help.
  • Service dogs can provide emotional support and stability. Learn about service dogs.

Support for Vulnerable Groups

  • LGBTQ+ people, women, and young people may face extra risks—specialist support is available.
  • Ask for women-only or LGBTQ+ friendly services if needed.
  • Advocacy and peer support can help you access your rights.

Trauma-Informed Care & Advocacy

  • Many people experiencing homelessness have a history of trauma. Services should be delivered in a way that promotes healing and avoids re-traumatisation.
  • If you have trouble accessing services or feel your rights are not respected, seek advocacy support from a local organisation or support worker.
  • You deserve respect and dignity, no matter your situation.

Where to Get Help

  • NHS Sexual Health Clinics: Free, confidential STI testing, contraception, and advice.
  • Rape Crisis: 0808 802 9999 (women & girls, support after sexual violence)
  • SARCs: NHS Sexual Assault Referral Centres for medical and psychological help.
  • Shelter: Housing and legal advice for anyone facing homelessness.
  • St Mungo's, Spectra, Terrence Higgins Trust, Brook, FPA: Specialist support for sexual health, HIV, and vulnerable groups.
