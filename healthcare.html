Healthcare Access

Resources for accessing healthcare services and managing health needs while experiencing homelessness.

Mental Health Support

Crisis Support

If you're experiencing a mental health crisis and need immediate support:

  • Call the Samaritans: 116 123 (24/7, free)
  • Text SHOUT to 85258 for crisis text support
  • Call the NHS: 111 and ask for mental health support
  • In an emergency, call 999 or go to A&E

Ongoing Support

Regular mental health support services available without a fixed address:

  • Homeless Mental Health Teams - Specialists who understand the unique challenges of homelessness
  • Drop-in Counselling - Many day centers offer free counselling sessions
  • Mind - Local branches offer support regardless of housing situation

Physical Health Care

Your Right to Healthcare

Everyone in the UK has the right to access NHS healthcare, regardless of housing status. You don't need a fixed address to register with a GP.

Mobile Health Services

Many cities have mobile healthcare units that visit homeless shelters and day centers:

  • Health Outreach Teams - Nurses and doctors who visit homeless services
  • Foot Care Clinics - Specialist care for foot problems which are common when homeless
  • Dental Services - Mobile dental units providing essential oral healthcare
  • Vision Care - Free eye tests and glasses through specialized services

Contact your local day center or shelter to find out when these services visit.

Medication Access & Management

Getting Prescriptions

If you don't have funds for prescription costs:

  • Apply for an HC2 certificate for free prescriptions
  • Ask your GP about the Minor Ailments Scheme for basic medications
  • Some day centers can help with prescription costs in emergencies

Storing Medication

If you have nowhere safe to store medications:

  • Some hostels offer secure medication storage
  • Day centers may provide lockers for medication
  • Ask your GP about daily dispensing options
  • For medicines requiring refrigeration, speak to your pharmacist about alternatives

Registering with a GP

Your Right to Register

GP practices cannot refuse to register you because you don't have a fixed address or identification. If you're having difficulties:

  • Use the address of a day center or hostel (with their permission)
  • Register as having "no fixed abode"
  • Ask a homeless healthcare team to help with registration
  • If refused, contact Patient Advice and Liaison Service (PALS)

Homeless-Friendly Practices

Some GP practices specialize in supporting homeless patients. Our support workers can help you find a suitable practice in your area and accompany you to appointments if needed.

Medical Emergencies

Emergency Services

In a medical emergency:

  • Call 999 for an ambulance
  • Go to your nearest A&E department
  • You have the right to emergency care regardless of your housing status

Non-Emergency Care

For non-emergency medical advice:

  • Call 111 - This service can advise on local care options
  • Visit a walk-in center or urgent care center
  • Speak to a pharmacist for minor health concerns

Substance Use & Recovery Services

Getting Help

Support services available for addiction recovery:

  • Drop-in services - No appointment needed at many centers
  • Needle exchange programs - Safe disposal and clean equipment
  • Community drug and alcohol teams - Specialized support
  • Detox programs - Medical support for withdrawal
  • Rehabilitation services - Longer-term recovery support

Self-Referral

You can self-refer to many drug and alcohol services without going through a GP. Contact local services directly or call the Frank helpline on 0300 123 6600 for confidential advice and referrals.

Additional Support

Our team can help with:

  • Accompanying you to medical appointments
  • Explaining medical information and treatment options
  • Advocating on your behalf with healthcare providers
  • Accessing specialist care and support
  • Setting up medication reminders and management systems
