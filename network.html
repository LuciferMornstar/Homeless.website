Support Network

Being part of a supportive community is essential for sustainable housing solutions. Our Support Network connects individuals overcoming homelessness with a wide range of resources, volunteers, and community partners.

Whether you're looking to receive support or volunteer your time and skills, our network offers a way to make meaningful connections.

After Housing Support

Continued Support Network

Getting housing is just the first step. We understand that many individuals feel isolated and abandoned after securing housing, when official support often ends.

Our network ensures that you remain connected to a community that cares. We provide ongoing support, social connections, and practical assistance to help you thrive in your new home.

Community Integration

Local meet-ups, community events and social gatherings to build your network

Buddy System

A dedicated volunteer provides regular check-ins and companionship

Home Maintenance

Help with household tasks and connecting to repair services

Life Skills Workshops

Ongoing education in budgeting, cooking, and home management

Join Our Volunteer Network

Our volunteers are the backbone of our support network. By joining our volunteer network, you can make a real difference in someone's life.

We offer various volunteer opportunities based on your interests, skills, and availability:

Buddy Program

Become a buddy to someone who has recently transitioned into housing, providing friendship and regular check-ins.

Skills Sharing

Share your expertise in budgeting, cooking, home maintenance, or other practical life skills.

Community Events

Help organize social events that build community and combat isolation.

Ready to join our network of volunteers? Get involved today!

Success Stories

Mark's Journey

"After finding housing, I felt more alone than ever. The support network connected me with my buddy, James, who helped me feel part of the community again. The weekly skills workshops gave me confidence in managing my new home, and now I'm even volunteering to help others."

Sarah's Story

"Being part of the network meant that when I faced a crisis with my heating in winter, I wasn't alone. My network connections helped me navigate repairs and provided temporary heating. Without this support, I might have lost my housing again."
