Need Shelter Tonight?

Emergency Accommodation

If you need somewhere to stay tonight:

  1. Call your local council's housing department immediately. Even outside office hours, many councils have emergency numbers.
  2. Call our emergency helpline for immediate advice and support.
  3. If you're sleeping rough, check local night shelters - many operate on a first-come, first-served basis.
  4. During winter months (November-March), additional emergency beds are often available through Severe Weather Emergency Protocol (SWEP).

Immediate Options

Council Emergency Housing

Your local council has a legal duty to help if you're homeless or at risk of homelessness. Contact them immediately.

Night Shelters

Providing basic overnight accommodation. Some operate on a first-come, first-served basis; others require referrals.

Severe Weather Provision

During cold weather, additional emergency beds are made available to anyone sleeping rough.

Domestic Abuse Refuges

If you're fleeing domestic abuse, specialist accommodation is available. Call the National Domestic Abuse Helpline.

Hostels

Providing longer-term accommodation with support services. Some may have beds available on an emergency basis.

Support Organizations

Charities like Shelter, Crisis, and local organizations can help you find emergency accommodation.

Key Emergency Advice

What to Expect When Seeking Emergency Help

If you contact your local council for emergency housing:

  1. Initial assessment - The council will ask about your situation to determine if you're eligible for emergency housing.
  2. Priority need assessment - They'll check if you're in "priority need" (e.g., if you have children, are pregnant, or vulnerable).
  3. Temporary accommodation - If eligible, they should provide temporary accommodation while they investigate further.
  4. Further investigation - They'll look into your case in more detail over the coming days.

What to Take With You

  • ID documents if you have them (passport, driving license, birth certificate)
  • Any evidence of your housing situation (eviction notice, tenancy agreement)
  • Evidence of income or benefits
  • Any medical evidence if you have health conditions
  • Details of any support workers or social workers
  • Information about your needs (e.g., medication, dietary requirements)

If You're Refused Help

If the council refuses to help, ask for the decision in writing and contact our helpline immediately. You have the right to challenge decisions, and we can help you do this.

Emergency Safety Tips

If You Have to Sleep Rough

While we always recommend seeking emergency accommodation, if you have no choice but to sleep rough:

  • Try to sleep in a visible, well-lit area where people pass by
  • If possible, stay with someone you trust
  • Keep your essential documents and medications close to you
  • Use the StreetLink app to connect with local outreach services
  • In cold weather, seek indoor spaces like 24-hour facilities when possible

Health Emergencies

If you're experiencing a medical emergency:

  • Call 999 for immediate medical attention
  • For non-emergency medical issues, visit an A&E department, walk-in center, or call 111
  • You have the right to register with a GP even if you don't have a fixed address
  • Hospital A&E departments cannot refuse treatment based on housing status
  • If you need mental health support, contact the Mental Health Crisis Line

Staying Safe in Crisis

General safety tips during crisis situations:

  • Keep your phone charged when possible - libraries and public buildings often have charging points
  • Share your location with a trusted person if you can
  • If you feel unsafe, seek well-lit public areas with people around
  • Trust your instincts - if a situation feels unsafe, try to leave
  • Know your rights - police and security guards should treat you with respect
