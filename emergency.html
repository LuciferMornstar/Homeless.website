Emergency Contacts

Important contacts and resources for crisis situations and emergency assistance.

Immediate Danger

Emergency Services

If you or someone else is in immediate danger, call:

CALL 999 (Emergency Services)

Call 999 for police, ambulance, or fire service if there is:

  • Immediate danger to life
  • Crime in progress
  • Serious injury
  • Violence or threat of violence

Mental Health Crisis

Urgent Mental Health Support

If you're experiencing a mental health crisis:

Samaritans: 116 123 (24/7 Support) Crisis Text Line: Text SHOUT to 85258

Additional Support Lines

  • NHS Mental Health Helpline: Call 111 and ask for mental health crisis support
  • CALM (Campaign Against Living Miserably): 0800 58 58 58 (5pm-midnight, 365 days a year)
  • Mind InfoLine: 0300 123 3393 (9am-6pm, Monday-Friday)
  • Papyrus (for people under 35): 0800 068 4141

There is always someone who will listen. You don't have to face this alone.

Emergency Shelter

Need Emergency Accommodation Tonight?

If you have nowhere to stay tonight:

Call Streetlink: 0808 800 4444

Streetlink connects people sleeping rough with local services. They operate across England and Wales.

Local Authority Emergency Housing

Your local council has a legal duty to help if you are:

  • Homeless or at risk of homelessness within 56 days
  • Eligible for assistance (based on immigration status)
  • In priority need (families with children, pregnant women, vulnerable due to age or health conditions)

The 56-Day Rule: Early Intervention Is Key

Under the Homelessness Reduction Act 2017, councils must help prevent your homelessness if you're at risk of losing your home within 56 days (8 weeks).

Don't wait until you're on the streets - seek help as soon as you receive an eviction notice, are asked to leave by family/friends, or know you can no longer afford your housing.

Contact your local council's housing department or visit your nearest council office during working hours.

Domestic Abuse

Domestic Abuse Support

If you are experiencing domestic abuse:

National Domestic Abuse Helpline: 0808 2000 247

This helpline is free and available 24/7. They can:

  • Provide confidential advice
  • Help arrange emergency accommodation
  • Connect you with local support services
  • Help develop safety plans

Men's Advice Line: 0808 801 0327 - For men experiencing domestic abuse

Note: If your internet usage might be monitored, consider using incognito/private browsing or clear your browser history after visiting this page.

Youth Support

Under 25 and Homeless or At Risk?

Specialized support for young people:

Centrepoint Helpline: 0808 800 0661 Runaway Helpline: 116 000

Additional Youth Support

  • Childline: 0800 1111 (for under 19s)
  • The Mix: 0808 808 4994 (for under 25s)

Young people often have specific rights to housing and support. These services can advise on your options.

Other Emergency Resources

Need Urgent Food

If you urgently need food:

  • Visit your local food bank (identification may not be required for emergency food parcels)
  • Contact your local community support services
  • Some day centers and shelters provide emergency food with no questions asked

Medical Emergencies

For urgent medical help:

  • Call 999 for life-threatening emergencies
  • Visit an A&E department for serious injuries or illness
  • Call 111 for urgent but non-emergency medical advice
  • Visit a walk-in center or urgent care center for minor injuries and illnesses

Cold Weather Emergency

Severe Weather Emergency Protocol (SWEP)

During extremely cold weather (usually when temperatures drop below 0°C), local councils activate emergency accommodation for anyone sleeping rough, regardless of eligibility, priority need, or local connection.

To access SWEP accommodation:

  • Contact your local council's housing department
  • Speak to local outreach teams who are active during SWEP
  • Call Streetlink on 0808 800 4444 to connect with local services

Note: SWEP accommodation is provided on a night-by-night basis during cold weather periods.
