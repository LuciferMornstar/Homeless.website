Housing & Shelter

56-Day Rule: Act Now, Don't Wait

Under the Homelessness Reduction Act 2017, you can get help before you become homeless.

If you're at risk of losing your home within 56 days (about 8 weeks), your local council has a legal duty to help prevent your homelessness.

When to Apply for Help

  • You've received an eviction notice
  • Your landlord has told you to leave
  • Your family/friends have asked you to move out
  • You're experiencing domestic abuse
  • You can no longer afford your rent or mortgage
  • You're staying somewhere temporarily with no right to remain

How To Apply for Housing Help

  1. Contact your local council's housing department - Find your local council at gov.uk/find-local-council
  2. Bring identification and evidence - Such as ID, eviction notice, or proof of your situation
  3. Complete a homeless application - The council must assess your situation
  4. Get your Personal Housing Plan - This will outline steps to help resolve your situation
  5. Follow the steps in your plan - Keep in regular contact with your housing officer

Food Support

Food Banks

Food banks provide emergency food supplies. Most require a referral, but many can also help in crisis situations without one.

How to Access a Food Bank:

  1. Contact your local Citizens Advice, GP, social worker, or school for a referral
  2. Once you have a referral, visit the food bank with your voucher
  3. You'll usually receive a package with approximately three days of nutritionally balanced food
Find Food Banks Near You

Other Food Support Options

  • Community fridges and pantries (pay what you can)
  • Soup kitchens and community meals
  • Religious organizations often provide meals
  • Mobile food services in some areas

No referral? Many areas have food support options that don't require referrals. Contact our helpline for information about options in your area.

Healthcare

Access to Healthcare

Everyone has the right to register with a GP, regardless of their housing situation. You do not need a fixed address or identification to register.

How to Access Healthcare:

  • Register with a GP using a temporary address (hostel, friend's house, or the GP practice address)
  • Visit walk-in centers or urgent care centers without an appointment
  • Access A&E for emergencies
  • Ask about healthcare outreach services for homeless people in your area

Remember: GP practices cannot refuse to register you because you do not have proof of address or immigration status. If they refuse, ask them to provide the refusal in writing and contact Healthwatch for support.

Financial Advice

Benefits & Financial Support

You may be entitled to benefits or financial support, even if you have no fixed address.

Available Support:

  • Universal Credit
  • Housing Benefit
  • Personal Independence Payment (PIP)
  • Job Seeker's Allowance
  • Emergency grants from local welfare assistance schemes

Getting Help with Claims:

  • Citizens Advice can help with benefit applications
  • Job Centers can support with Universal Credit claims
  • Housing support workers at shelters can assist with forms

No fixed address? You can use a 'care of' address for benefits, such as a day center, hostel, or trusted friend's address. Some Job Centers also allow you to use their address.

Mental Health Support

Mental Health Services

Your mental health matters. Support is available regardless of your housing situation.

Support Services:

  • Talk to your GP about mental health concerns
  • Contact Samaritans (116 123) for 24/7 emotional support
  • Text SHOUT to 85258 for crisis text support
  • Many homelessness organizations have specialist mental health workers
  • Community mental health teams can provide support
Mental Health Resources

Substance Use Support

Support is available if you're struggling with alcohol or drug use.

Getting Help:

  • Talk to your GP about referral to drug and alcohol services
  • Contact local addiction support services
  • Frank (0300 123 6600) provides drug information and advice
  • Alcoholics Anonymous and Narcotics Anonymous offer peer support

Next Steps

Creating Your Personal Action Plan

Everyone's situation is different. Here's a simple framework to help you navigate your next steps:

  1. Address immediate needs first:
    • Safe place to stay tonight
    • Food and water
    • Urgent medical care
    • Safety from harm
  2. Connect with specialist support:
    • Local council housing department (especially if within 56 days of homelessness)
    • Homelessness charities and outreach teams
    • Citizens Advice for benefits and legal guidance
  3. Stabilize your situation:
    • Secure temporary accommodation
    • Apply for appropriate benefits
    • Register with a GP
    • Obtain essential items (clothing, toiletries)
  4. Build for the future:
    • Work with support workers on long-term housing
    • Address underlying factors (employment, health, skills)
    • Connect with community support networks
    • Focus on personal wellbeing and recovery

Remember the 56-Day Rule

If you're facing housing issues, don't wait until you have nowhere to go. Early intervention is key. The council must help if you're at risk of homelessness within 56 days.

Our team can help you develop a tailored plan for your specific situation. You don't have to figure everything out alone.
