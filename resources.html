Homelessness Resources in the UK

Shelter

Shelter provides expert advice, support, and legal assistance for housing and homelessness issues. They are a secular organization dedicated to helping everyone in need.

Contact: 0808 800 4444

Visit Shelter

Crisis

Crisis offers education, training, and support to help people out of homelessness. They are a secular organization focused on long-term solutions.

Contact: 0300 636 1967

Visit Crisis

StreetLink

StreetLink connects rough sleepers with local services that can help them. This secular service ensures immediate help reaches those in need.

Contact: 0300 500 0914

Visit StreetLink

The Salvation Army

The Salvation Army is a Christian organization providing shelter, food, and support services. They operate shelters and community programs across the UK.

Contact: 020 7367 4500

Visit Salvation Army

Mental Health Resources

Mind

Mind provides advice and support to empower anyone experiencing a mental health problem. They are a secular organization offering helplines, local support groups, and online resources.

Contact: 0300 123 3393

Visit Mind

Samaritans

Samaritans offers a safe space to talk about mental health issues. Their trained volunteers are available 24/7 to listen and provide support. They are a secular organization.

Contact: 116 123 (free)

Visit Samaritans

Rethink Mental Illness

Rethink provides support and information for people living with mental illness. They are a secular organization offering advocacy services, crisis support, and advice.

Contact: 0808 801 0525

Visit Rethink

NHS Mental Health Services

The NHS offers free mental health services, including therapy, counseling, and crisis intervention. You can access these services through your GP.

Contact: 111 for non-emergencies

Visit NHS Mental Health

Homeless Mental Health Support

Homelessness can have a significant impact on mental health. Many individuals face anxiety, depression, and trauma as a result of their experiences. Our network provides specialized support to address these challenges, including access to counseling, peer support groups, and crisis intervention services.

If you or someone you know is struggling, reach out to one of the organizations listed above or contact our support team for guidance. Remember, seeking help is a sign of strength, not weakness.

Help with Pets When Homeless

Dogs Trust Hope Project

The Dogs Trust Hope Project provides free veterinary care for dogs belonging to homeless people. They also offer advice on finding pet-friendly accommodation.

Contact: 020 7837 0006

Visit Dogs Trust

StreetVet

StreetVet is a secular organization offering free veterinary care to pets of homeless individuals. Their services include vaccinations, microchipping, and treatment for illnesses.

Contact: Available through their website

Visit StreetVet

Our Pet Support Services

At Homeless Helpers, we understand that pets are family. We offer several services to help you keep your pet with you during difficult times:

  • Assistance Dog Certification: Help with getting your companion animal recognized as a service or emotional support animal
  • Pet-Friendly Shelter Directory: Information on shelters and housing that accept pets
  • Emergency Pet Food Bank: Access to food and supplies for your pet
  • Temporary Pet Fostering: Short-term care for your pet while you secure stable housing

Practical Services

Safe Medication Storage

Many shelters and community centers offer secure storage for medications. This ensures that your medication is kept safe and accessible when you need it.

If you are unsure where to store your medication, contact your local shelter or healthcare provider for advice.

Healthcare Resources

Receiving Post

Receiving mail without a fixed address can be challenging, but there are solutions:

  • Post Office Poste Restante service allows you to collect mail from a local post office
  • Some shelters let you use their address for correspondence
  • Community centers may offer mailbox services
