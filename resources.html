Resources for Support

At Homeless Helpers, we provide a wide range of resources to support individuals and families experiencing homelessness. Our goal is to empower you with the tools and connections needed to achieve stability and independence.

Explore the resources below to find the support you need. Whether you are looking for emergency assistance, long-term solutions, or mental health support, we have compiled comprehensive information to guide you.

Homelessness Resources in the UK

Shelter

Shelter provides expert advice, support, and legal assistance for housing and homelessness issues. They are a secular organization dedicated to helping everyone in need. Visit Shelter or call their helpline at 0808 800 4444.

Crisis

Crisis offers education, training, and support to help people out of homelessness. They are a secular organization focused on long-term solutions. Visit Crisis or call 0300 636 1967.

StreetLink

StreetLink connects rough sleepers with local services that can help them. This secular service ensures immediate help reaches those in need. Visit StreetLink or call 0300 500 0914.

The Salvation Army

The Salvation Army is a Christian organization providing shelter, food, and support services. They operate shelters and community programs across the UK. Visit The Salvation Army or call 020 7367 4500.

Mental Health Resources

Mind

Mind provides advice and support to empower anyone experiencing a mental health problem. They are a secular organization offering helplines, local support groups, and online resources. Visit Mind or call their helpline at 0300 123 3393.

Samaritans

Samaritans offers a safe space to talk about mental health issues. Their trained volunteers are available 24/7 to listen and provide support. They are a secular organization. Call 116 123 for free or visit Samaritans.

Rethink Mental Illness

Rethink provides support and information for people living with mental illness. They are a secular organization offering advocacy services, crisis support, and advice. Visit Rethink or call 0808 801 0525.

NHS Mental Health Services

The NHS offers free mental health services, including therapy, counseling, and crisis intervention. You can access these services through your GP or by visiting NHS Mental Health.

Help with Pets When Homeless

Dogs Trust Hope Project

The Dogs Trust Hope Project provides free veterinary care for dogs belonging to homeless people. They also offer advice on finding pet-friendly accommodation. Visit Dogs Trust or call 020 7837 0006.

StreetVet

StreetVet is a secular organization offering free veterinary care to pets of homeless individuals. Their services include vaccinations, microchipping, and treatment for illnesses. Visit StreetVet.

Homeless Mental Health Support

Homelessness can have a significant impact on mental health. Many individuals face anxiety, depression, and trauma as a result of their experiences. Our network provides specialized support to address these challenges, including access to counseling, peer support groups, and crisis intervention services.

If you or someone you know is struggling, reach out to one of the organizations listed above or contact our support team for guidance. Remember, seeking help is a sign of strength, not weakness.

Storing Medication and Receiving Post

Safe Medication Storage

Many shelters and community centers offer secure storage for medications. This ensures that your medication is kept safe and accessible when you need it. If you are unsure where to store your medication, contact your local shelter or healthcare provider for advice.

Receiving Post

Receiving mail without a fixed address can be challenging, but there are solutions. Services like Post Office Poste Restante allow you to collect mail from a local post office. Some shelters also let you use their address for correspondence. Check with your local shelter or community center for more information.
