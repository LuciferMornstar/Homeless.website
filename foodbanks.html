How to Access Food Support

Food Bank Referrals

Most food banks work on a referral system. You can get referred by:

  • Citizen's Advice Bureau
  • GP or health visitor
  • Social worker or support worker
  • Local council welfare team
  • School staff or children's centers

Emergency Food Without Referral

If you need food urgently and can't get a referral:

  • Some independent food banks don't require referrals
  • Community kitchens and soup kitchens usually provide meals without referrals
  • Local religious organizations often provide emergency food assistance
  • For immediate help, call our helpline: 0800 123 4567

Food Vouchers & Financial Support

Available Support

Various schemes can help with food costs:

  • Healthy Start Vouchers: For pregnant women and families with children under 4
  • Free School Meals: For eligible school-age children
  • Local Welfare Assistance: Emergency support from your local council
  • Household Support Fund: Help with essentials including food

How To Apply

Our support workers can help you apply for:

  • Universal Credit advance payments
  • Hardship payments
  • Local welfare assistance schemes
  • Charity grants for food and essentials

Special Dietary Requirements

Medical & Religious Dietary Needs

If you have specific dietary requirements due to health conditions, allergies, or religious beliefs:

  • Mention your dietary needs when getting a food bank referral
  • Call ahead to check if a food bank can accommodate your needs
  • Some food banks have specialized lists for different dietary requirements
  • Religious dietary needs (halal, kosher, etc.) can often be accommodated with advance notice

Food Allergies & Health Conditions

Food services that can accommodate specific health-related dietary needs:

  • Gluten-free options
  • Dairy-free alternatives
  • Low-sodium meals for those with high blood pressure
  • Diabetic-friendly food parcels

Contact our support team for help finding food services that meet your specific dietary requirements.
